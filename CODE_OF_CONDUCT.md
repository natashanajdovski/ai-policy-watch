# Code of Conduct

AI Policy Watch is a public-interest project. The mission — making federal
AI policy visible and non-partisan — requires that contributors of
different political views can collaborate here.

## The expectation

- **Good faith.** Assume other contributors are trying to help. Ask before
  you accuse.
- **Stay on the work.** Disagreements about a bill, a keyword, or a design
  choice are welcome. Disagreements that become about people are not.
- **Non-partisan framing.** In issues, PRs, and commit messages, describe
  bills and policies in source-mirroring language. Avoid "pro-innovation,"
  "anti-worker," and similar editorializing — even if you believe it's true.
- **English is fine, but plain English is better.** Assume your reader is a
  curious member of the public, not a lawyer.

## What we won't tolerate

- Harassment, personal attacks, slurs, or sustained disruptive behavior
- Doxxing or sharing private information of other contributors
- Using the project to promote a political candidate or cause
- Sockpuppet accounts, vote brigading, or other manipulation

## Enforcement

The maintainer will remove comments, commits, issues, or PRs that don't
meet these expectations, and may ban accounts that repeatedly violate them.
Reports can be sent via a private GitHub message to the maintainer listed
in [CODEOWNERS](.github/CODEOWNERS).

This code applies in all project spaces — issues, PRs, commit messages,
discussions, and any in-person or external spaces that represent the
project.
